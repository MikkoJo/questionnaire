{% trans 'Kyselyn toteuttaja ja tutkijat' %}

{% blocktrans %} The survey is carried out as a cooperation of Aalto University (Helsinki, Finland) and the BeautifulPB-initiative. Aalto University has been developing the softGIS-method since 2005 in cooperation with Finnish cities interested in participatory urban planning. Recently it carried out a project with Japan and Australia, and this survey is the first time that the softGIS is tested in the US. The results of the survey will be utilized by the BeautifulPB and other interested parties to better understand how PB residents use the area and how they would like to see it developing in the future. The results may also be used by the Finnish research team to analyse the perceived environmental quality and everyday mobility issues. Marketta Kyttä and Kaisa Schmidt-Thomé (kaisa.schmidt-thome(at)aalto.fi) from Aalto University are in charge of the research. The contact persons of the Beautiful PB for the survey are Kristen Victor /kristen(at)sustainabilitymatters.us) and Sara Berns (sara(at)pacificbeach.org). {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} The offer of Aalto University to test the softGIS in Pacific Beach is linked to a European Union research exchange programme and its project called CLUDs. Within the CLUDs it has been possible to invite several researchers from Finland, UK and Italy to be visiting scholars at the San Diego State University, School of Public Affairs. However, introducing the questionnaire in Pacific Beach is not an official part of the CLUDs but rather a joint effort of Aalto University and the BeautifulPB. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Tietosuoja' %}

{% blocktrans %} The softGIS questionnaire is situated on a secure server at Aalto University. The collected database is accessible only to researchers, but they may pass the final database to the BeautifulPB initiative after removing the data on home locations. The researchers will not visualise or otherwise present data in a manner that could enable connecting a response with a certain household. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Research data will be analysed in groups, not on an individual level. The results are grouped by e.g. gender, age and neighbourhood, and they deal with the average response given by the groups, for example the average distance to important places or travel modes to work. It is impossible to track individuals or their answers from any results that will be published. {% endblocktrans %}
